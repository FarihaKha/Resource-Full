Support Groups

Your mental wellbeing is just as important as physical wellness. Surround yourself with those that understand you and care about you. Below you will find some resources to help with this.

BIPOC BASED

Zencare is geared towards providing therapists focused on POC. They have sections focused on connecting POC therapists with other POC clients, because therapy seekers may feel as though someone with the same background will understand their problems.

South Asian Women - Domestic Violence is a nonprofit that focuses on providing help and additional resources for South Asian women, especially those of diaspora.

Youth Empowerment Program (YEP) focuses on helping youth utilize social skills in order to further develop one of three asian based programs created to help the AAPI community.

Sikh Coalition works to protect the constitutional right to practice your faith without fear.

MekongNYC is a social justice organization that brings dignity and value to the lives of Southeast Asians in the Bronx and throughout New York City.

Arab-American Family Support Center is a non-profit that empowers immigrants and refugees with the tools they need to successfully acclimate to the world around them and become active participants in their communities.

OCANYC is a civil rights organization promoting and protecting the political, economic, and cultural rights of Asian Pacific Americans in America.

Indo-Carribean Alliance is a nonprofit formed to serve the South Asians and Indo-Carribeans based in Queens through various programs.

The Black Institute enacts justice for communities of color and confronts barriers to social, racial, and economic inclusion.

AIBL is a nonprofit organization focused on supporting future Native American leaders.

Hispanic Federation Has a lot of resources for Hispanic youth and focuses on strengthening Latinx institutions.

SACNAS is an inclusive organization dedicated to fostering the success of Chicanos/Hispanics and Native American in STEM.

AAPCI is a nonprofit organization committed to reducing homelessness and addressing the related issues of domestic violence, HIV/AIDS, mental illness, substance abuse in the AA community

Project Reach is a non-profit dedicated to youth development and the cultural and economic revitalization of the Hunts Point section of the South Bronx

Teens Take Charge is a student-led movement for educational equity in New York City.

BIT is a global platform that enhances and enriches Black voices in technology.

QUEER RESOURCES

PFLAG-NYC is a partnership of parents, allies, and LGBT people working to make a better future for LGBT youth and adults.

MASGD works to support, empower, and connect LGBTQ+ Muslims.

QUIP focuses on providing resources, both mental and physical, and well as news for queer immigrants.

HOTT is a program designed specifically to meet the medical and mental health needs of LGBTQ adolescents and young adults.

QTPOC Meditation is a bipoc-only space that highlights queer trans/gender non-conforming creatives of color.
