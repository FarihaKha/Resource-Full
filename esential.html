Essential Items

One of the resources that are very important for all BIPOC communities are essential items such as food and shelter. Minorities experience starvation and homelessness at a much higher rate compared to white New Yorkers, which is why we are highlighting a few resources located in New York that can help. Please donate to the organizations you want to support!

Food

Access NYC has an emergency food assistance program. The best part of this program is that it is for everyone (that's right, everyone!), no matter what your immigration status is or how much money you have.


Do you live near a school? The NYCDOE gives free “grab & go” meals. There are even certain locations that provide kosher, halal, and vegetarian meals. Be sure to swing by to a school near you on weekdays from 9AM to 1PM!


Do you live in Bedford-Stuyvesant? If so, then you should check out Bed-Stuy Strong, a mutual aid group where everyone is dedicated to helping out their community, from food to even vaccines!


Another way to get food from Bed-Stuy is from the Brooklyn Rescue Mission, a non-profit that offers food to people in need, especially those suffering from the evolving COVID-19 crisis on Tuesdays and Wednesdays from 10AM to 12PM.


An amazing non-profit that you should support is Rethink Food. Rethink Food is committed to providing nutritious meals and stopping the widespread food insecurity. They are located in Manhattan, Brooklyn, the Bronx, Queens, Chicago, Nashville, and San Francisco.


Another non-profit that is dedicated to fighting this food insecurity is Invisible Hands. Ever since the COVID-19 crisis, so many people have been unable to receive food. That is why Invisible Hands delivers groceries, prescriptions, and more to those who are in need, including the elderly, immunocompromised, sick, and people with disabilities.


At Queens Together, independent restaraunts support each other to fight hunger for communities in the COVID-19 crisis. They want to "bring Queens together."


Heart of Dinner is an wonderful non-profit that serves meals, produce, and ingredients to East Asian elders in Lower Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens.


The Okra Project is a mutual aid collective that aims to provide healthy, culturally specific meals for Black Trans people.


Food Bank For New York City is a great non-profit that serves food to low-income New Yorkers. Their mission is to end food poverty!


Fuel The People. Fuel The Revolution. Fuel The People is a non-profit that provides food for protestors, supports local Black and POC-owned restaraunts and businesses, and donates to local organizations that support Black liberation.


Shelter

Coalition For the Homeless is an organization that makes sure anyone in New York can find help. Not only does it focus on housing, but it also has programs on crisis prevention, food, job training, youth, and advocay.


New Alternatives For LGBT Homeless Youth is a non-profit committed to taking care of the homeless LGBTQ+ youth. They provide meals, hygeine supplies, emergency clothing, MetroCards, urgent referrals for shelter and primary care, and psychiatric support.


Trinity Place Shelter is a homeless shelter for LGBTQ youth and young adults with a safe place to sleep, shower, eat, and store belongings. The shelter provides case management, education, career counseling, access to comprehensive health services, art classes, and mental health services.


The Ali Forney Center is a community center that provides support and services for LGBT youth to help them have healthy, independent lives.


Violence Intervention Program is a Latina-led organization that provides housing, economic justice, crisis counseling, advocacy, and support to survivors of domestic and sexual violence.
